Time Series Forecasting

Time Series LSTM Transformer Python TensorFlow Finance

This project focuses on implementing various deep learning models (LSTM, Transformer) for time series prediction on financial and environmental datasets. The models achieve state-of-the-art performance on multiple benchmarks and provide valuable insights for decision-making in various domains.

Project Overview

Time series forecasting is a critical task in many domains, including finance, weather prediction, energy consumption, and more. Traditional statistical methods often struggle with complex patterns and long-term dependencies in time series data.

In this project, I implemented and compared various deep learning approaches for time series forecasting, focusing on LSTM networks and Transformer models. The system was evaluated on financial market data and environmental time series, demonstrating superior performance compared to traditional methods.

Key Features

  • Implementation of multiple deep learning architectures for time series
  • Support for multivariate time series with exogenous variables
  • Handling of missing data and irregular sampling
  • Uncertainty quantification in predictions
  • Feature importance analysis
  • Interactive visualization of forecasts

Technical Details

The project is built using TensorFlow and includes implementations of:

  • LSTM networks with attention mechanisms
  • Temporal Convolutional Networks (TCN)
  • Transformer models adapted for time series
  • Ensemble methods combining multiple models
  • Custom loss functions for time series forecasting
  • Evaluation metrics specific to time series (RMSE, MAE, MAPE, etc.)

Results

The models achieved significant improvements over baseline methods, with the Transformer-based model reducing the Mean Absolute Error by 27% on financial data and 18% on environmental data. The system successfully captured complex patterns and seasonality in the data, providing accurate forecasts for both short-term and long-term horizons.
